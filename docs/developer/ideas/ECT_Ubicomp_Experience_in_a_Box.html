ECT Ubicomp Experience in a Box (tm)

Chris Greenhalgh, 2005-09-14

Introduction

Still following the thread with physical configuration, but trying to look a bit broader/at a slant, by asking how we could technically support the packaging, distribution and (locally-tailored) deployment/replication of ubicomp experiences...

In EQUATOR we have talked about the "EQUATOR Starter Kit" and similar ideas - a literal box of stuff that you might order from (say) Amazon, to get you started with building and giving EQUATOR-style Ubicomp Experiences. What would be in such a box? How would it work? How would you extend it? and so on...

Example Scenario/Thought Experiment

As a starting point we might imagine that the box contains:

  • A small infrastructure box (a "server", incorporating infrastructure wireless communications facility);
  • A bag of sensors, actuators and displays;
  • A bag of batteries and a recharger :-)
  • A bag of tags (RFID, barcode, etc.), labels, ties, etc.;
  • A mobile wearable or handheld device (and/or a "starter kit" (CF card?!) for your phone/PDA);
  • A handheld ubicomp multimeter/universal tool and/or a CDROM-based starter application for your PC;
  • A "getting started" DVD;
  • A 1-page "quick start" guide.

The quick-start guide explains how to turn on the ubimeter (or PC starter application). The ubimeter suggests you start with a level 1 (easy) experience; you choose from the list "phicon-controlled room lighting"...

  • It shows you how to plug in and turn on the infrastructure box.
  • The ubimeter detects/discoveres this as it comes online and giudes you through establishing the trust relationship between the ubimeter and the server if not preconfigured (e.g. physical docking and/or synchronized actions and/or user entry of (say) visible keys).
  • The ubimeter talks you through connecting the X10 controller and the Phidget RFID reader to the infrastructure box.
  • The ubimeter helps you to identify an X10 lamp/appliance module, and helps you to allocate a suitable house and device ID to it.
  • The ubimeter talks you through inserting the module to control one of your lamps, and asks you to identify (name and/or photograph) the lamp associated with that module.
    • The ubimeter stores the association between that lamp and the X10 ID.
  • The ubimeter allows you to add X10 controllers to more lamps.
  • The ubimeter asks you to choose a couple of small artefacts (e.g. non-precious ornaments) to become phicons.
  • The ubimeter asks you to attach an RFID tag to the first artefact and then place it on the reader; it asks you identify the artefact by name and/or by taking a photo of it with the ubimeter's built-in camera.
    • The ubimeter stores the association between that artefact and the read RFID tag value.
    • Alternatively, the kit may come with some artefacts with integrated RFIDs, which are already configured.
  • The ubimeter allows you to enroll more phicons.
  • Whenever a phicon is on the reader the ubimeter gives you the option of the configuring the lighting associated with that phicon:
    • it gives you a list of the known lamps (your own names and/or images), and their state/brightness, and allows you to change it.
    • you can also undo any changes.
  • The ubimeter allows you to archive the whole arrangement, and selectively discard elements of it
    • such as the mapping between phicons and lighting settings, or the presence and/or associations of X10 modules and /or RFID tags.
Extension 1:
  • You select the ubimeter option "Ubicomp-enable my phone"
  • The ubimeter installs (talks you through installing) an ECT MIDlet on your phone
    • and gathers various information about your phone's capabilities (e.g. bluetooth)
    • and establishes the trust relationship with the phone
  • You now select the experience extension "Remote control with <my phone>".
  • The ubimeter helps you to check bluetooth is enabled on your phone and the ECT MIDlet is running.
  • The ubimeter pushes the necessary configuration to the phone MIDlet.
  • The user selects the experience trigger option "when i am in range".
  • The ECT MIDlet now checks periodically if it is range of the installation, and when it is - assuming the relevant experience is actuve - offers the user access to a GUI to check the current phicon and modify the light levels for the current phicon.
Variant 2:
  • A friend visits who has their own installation. They have written a "disco" extension which is on their phone.
  • When they walk into my house my ubimeter offers me the option of allowing them to exchange information. I allow this.
  • Their disco extension determines that it is compability with my current installation, and offers an option to activate it.
  • My friend activates it.
  • My ubimeter asks me to confirm if I will allow it to interface to my installation; I confirm.
  • The light flash in time to my friend's MP3 (well not very in time with X10 :-)
Variant 3:
  • My friend visits again with their Ubicomp-enabled phone.
  • I have now configured my system to recommend mood music associated with each phicon.
  • When my friend is in range, their phone asks if they want to receive music recommendations from my installation; the accept.
  • Suitable music is suggested to my friends phone, which it plays
    • some of it is already on the phone; some is streamed from my installation; some is bought by him when it is recommended
Comments:
  • can i test/simulate it?
  • how do i author "content" for it?
  • the ubimeter embodies the locus of control/management, all be it from outside the experience "run-time". Is this a good real strategy? [its a bit like the hitchhiker's guide to the galaxy :o) ]
  • some negotiation/coordination is at the level of "experiences" (like "phicon-based lighting-control"); other is lower level (?) (e.g. "X10 devices present", or "suggested stream")
