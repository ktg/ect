ECT "semantics" scenario: content, "significance" and interaction 

Chris Greenhalgh 2005-07-20

Introduction

This is a sister-document to ECT_semantics_physical_configuration.html.

This document consider the extension of the "raw" information in ECT components and properties to support the definitions of content and interaction, and in particular explores the notion of "significance" and its potential utility/interpretation.

So... I have been doing some work on ECT, and trying to thing of suitable demonstration applications, and been struggling mightily to do so. This may be because ECT is actually not very useful or appropriate. However I prefer to think at the moment that it is because the software component/property aspect/view which currently predominates in ECT is not a good fit for notions of "application" or "content" (although it is useful at its own particuar "level" in the system).

Following from the above-mentioned exploration of modelling and making use of  information about the physical organisation of the system, this note explores a similar extension to more directly model "content" and "significance" in and around ECT as it currently stands. This is also responding to reflections on other EQUATOR experiences, including MacRoom, Augurscope, Unearthing Virtual History, Skipping, (my perception of) Chawton, POUTs, ...

Definitions

Some initial definitions/explorations:

  • "Significance" is concerned with both
    • significant (to be taken note of) versus insignificant (to be ignored) and
    • signification, i.e. what it stands for or represents.
  • Behaviour is generally about what the system does.
    • Observed behaviour is what it has been seen (or otherwise observed) to do in particular historical situations/occasions
    • Future, specified or programmed behaviour will typically be expressed in terms of sets of possible behaviours contingent on the activities/behaviours of other aspects of the "total system", including users and physical environment.
    • The behaviour (or application) will often be specified (or at least initially envisioned) in terms of the "significant" actions of users, arrangements of physical artefacts and environment, etc.
      • This may or may not be refined or recast in terms of system elements: sensors, programmed behaviours,  actuators, etc. (the translation may be tacet/implicit)
  • Interaction is about how the system responds to its users
    • This is a particular view of behaviour which emphasises action & response and/or dialog.
  • Content is about what the users gets (back)
    • It is probably expressed as particular families of contingency and behaviour/response, and may include media elements and other forms of information which may be "communicated" to user(s).
    • It may be authored, i.e. crafted by one or more people with particular responsibility for this.
    • It probably assumes a particular stereotype of behaviour/interaction. This can be embodied in a software "engine" of some sort.
    • It is typically (recursively) divisible into smaller parts, each of which is typically deemed to be appropriate or applicable only in certain circumstances or for certain purposes. 
    • Some forms of content are (taking illustrative examples from a first-person shooter game :-):
      • primarily passive, just for presenting to the user (in some way), e.g. media assets: geometries, textures, images, text
      • organisational, e.g. initial content (monster, trap, etc.) placement maps
      • constraints, for users and/or system elements, e.g. reachability maps, physics (could be treated as behaviour??)
      • behaviours, to be enacted by elements of the system, e.g. animations (could be treated as passive?!), traps, attacks, unlocking remote doors

Notes

  • The design and construction of the physical elements of the system is a key activity in itself, which has a major contribution to the sense and "feel" (the significance?!) of the final system.
  • The content/engine disjunction reflects the specialisation of skills and roles between "content developers" or "artists" and "programmers", which is analogous to the levels of engagement discussed with ECT.
    • We do not yet have a "content developer" level as such, since this would be tied to specific components (I guess; or new perspectives as being explored here); c.f. scripting.
  • How general are the stereotypes for interaction/content? Should one or more be directly supported by the system? If not, how is this perspective added/integrated?

Examples of application/behaviour/interaction stereotypes in ubicomp

  • presentation/filtering of "contextually relevant" information
    • esp. location-relevant, automatically, on a personal display
      • e.g. touring guide, museum guide
      • e.g. interruption management
    • including localised task support
      • e.g. navigation, wayfinding (GPS navigation system)
      • e.g. maintainance (photocopier :-), crisis/disaster activities
  • capture of local (esp. physical world) information
    • recording and recall
      • e.g. prosthetic memory, smart-camera, memories for life, contextual reminders
      • e.g. surveying, scene-of-crime, site-specific planning
      • e.g. authoring
    • remote monitoring and anomaly detection
      • e.g. elder-care, health-care, environmental monitoring, security (prison or fortress)
      • also e.g. "awareness" [less intrusive/oppressive?!]
  • device/location-independent interaction
    • e.g. mobile (software) interfaces and applications, payment?!, mobile/distributed access to applications/services/processes, e.g. food ordering
    • e.g. ensemble/distributed interaction (esp. ad hoc/dynamically configured), using nearby interfaces with personal devices, offloading personal storage/computation, personal-area networks/convergence
  • augmented (physical) artefacts and environments
    • artefacts, e.g. phicons, tagged paper, AR (CYSMN?!), smart-its chemical containers
    • systems, e.g. tangible interfaces
    • envionrments, e.g. responsive environments, art installations, "smart" home
  • social/collaborative
    • communication
      • e.g. telephony
      • virtual co-location (and collaboration), e.g. CYSMN, TheMacRoom
    • social prosthesis
      • e.g. p2p "jewelery" for introduction/identification, wireless game trading/gift extensions
      • more general brokering?

Examples of "significance" and sensing

"Significant" action/interaction inferred from sensor readings (component property values):
  • Pinger receiver reports pinger ID I (e.g. Ambient Wood, Mac-to-street, Chawton)
    • => Pinger, hence device (hence person?!) "close to" physical location where (a) pinger with ID I deployed (at time T)
      • => person (device) "has gone to" physical location(s) associated with pinger ID I
  • GPS position XYZ (e.g. Unearthing etc.)
    • => GPS received, hence device (hence person?!) "roughly at lat/long (relative to the earth)" XYZ (at time T)
      • => person "has gone to" XYZ
      • => person "has gone to" significant physical location L (position ~XYZ)
  • Ultrasonic receiver reports position XYZ (e.g. MacRoom, CuriousHome?)
    • => Ultrasonic receiver (transmitter) (hence device...) "in range of" ("close to"?) (particular) transmitter (receiver) array (at time T)
    • => Ultrasonic receiver (transmitter) (hence device...) "roughtly at offset" XYZ relative to the (particular) transmitter (receiver) array (at time T)
      • => person "is near to" ("is looking at"?!) exhibit E (which has location ~XYZ)
  • Bluetooth discovery of MAC address M (e.g. not-so-curious home?)
    • => Bluetooth radio, (hence device (phone, PDA), hence person??...) "close to" physical location where polling bluetooth radio deployed (at time T)
  • RFID reader reports RFID R (e.g. shape castle, skipping)
    • => RFID tag having ID R hence (particular) physical artefact "close to" physical location of (particular?) aerial of (particular) RFID reader
      • => physical artefact (having RFID tag R) "has been placed on"(?!) physical artefact (housing aerial of RFID reader) (by person P??)
  • analog input (connected to dial) changes value to V (e.g. audio chair, shape virtual radio, group proj. game)
    • => someone "manipates" dial to angle A(V) 
      • small delta-V may be taken to be "noise" (electrical, mechanical, finger vibration)
    • => someone "selects (e.g.) channel" C (angle ~A) for device associated with (that) dial.
    • => A>threshold: someone "opens the door" (group proj. game; analog input corresponds to angle of a door handle)
  • Load cell array report values ABCD (e.g. weight table, skipping)
    • => device which integrates those load cells (table) "is carrying weight" ("is subject to force") M(ABCD)
    • => mass (centre of gravity) "roughly at offset" XY(ABCD) relative to the (particular) load cells/load sensing surface
  • digital input (connected to a pressure pad) is true (e.g. audio chair)
    • => something "is on" the physical artefact on/under which the pressure pad is placed
      • => (e.g.) someone (?!) "is sitting on" the audio chair
  • digital input (connected to a button) becomes true (e.g. shared lights)
    • => someone "presses" the (particular, e.g. green) button
      • someone (e.g.) "(wants to) make brighter" the light associated with that button

Note: many "significant" interactions are not sense-able (either in some particular context, or in general), e.g.

  • is someone actually "looking at" a particular exhibit (or device)?
    • are the "attending to" or "interested in" that particular exhibit?
    • is someone actually "thinking about" something particular
  • is someone (currently) alone? having a conversation?
  • is the reading in error for some (external) reason (e.g. reflections)?
  • is someone lost? or having trouble?
    • or are they (say) waiting for traffic to pass? or sheltering from the rain?
  • is someone near to some thing/place which is much smaller than the positioning system's resolution or accuracy?
Some "significant" things in relation to application stereotypes:
  • presentation/filtering of "contextually relevant" information
    • where are you
    • what is your (notionally sensable) "context" (e.g. other people, history of activities)
    • what are your interests/other non-sensed "context" (e.g. appointments, plans)
    • what information is available
  • capture of local (esp. physical world) information
    • what is the state of the world/environment
    • what is happening
      • also inferred activities, people, etc.
    • is it "normal"
  • device/location-independent interaction
    • what devices are to hand/nearby
    • what are their capabilities, communications, etc.
    • what applications/services are "in play", with what requirements, etc.
  • augmented (physical) artefacts and environments
    • which artefact is it
    • what is the user "input"
    • what is happening...
  • social/collaborative
    • who/what kind of person do you want to communicate/share with
    • who is nearby - physically/virtually
